Computer Games

Hiram Abif's Artefact

Do Freemason play computer games? Well...., yes, after all, we are also humans.

Do not expect that Hiram Abif's Artefact game will disclose our secrets to you. Quite contrary, it is based on completely fabricated story.

However, it has several masonic overtones. In particular:
  • It teaches a long forgotten skill of performing calculations on a stick.
  • It teaches mathematics, one of the liberal arts, which we, indeed, practice.
  • It promotes learning and self-improvement, which is what Freemasonry is all about.
  • The story-line references ancient times, which has a lot to do with our tradition.
  • Knight Templars now exist as a separate degree in Freemasonry.
  • It touches the topic of alchemy, which some of our members are interested in.
  • Nicolaus Copernicus, who appears in the game, is the parton of our lodge.
  • The game requires patience and discipline of mind.
The game has been constructed using Mozilla's Firefox browser. It will also work on Google's Chrome. Use the link below to proceed to the game.
